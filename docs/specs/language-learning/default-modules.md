# Default Module List — Danish Language Learning
**Target:** Professional fluency (C2) for director-level work in a Danish company
**Learner profile:** Native English speaker, starting at A1/A2
**Total modules:** ~123 across 6 CEFR levels

Each module entry includes:
- **Theme** — broad topic area
- **Communication goal** — what you can do after completing it
- **Grammar focus** — key concepts introduced or practised
- **Vocabulary focus** — the word/phrase domain

---

## A1 — Foundation (12 modules)
*You already have some A1 exposure. These modules are designed to be moved through quickly — they consolidate the foundation before A2 builds on it.*

---

### A1-01 — Who Are You?
- **Theme:** Identity & introductions
- **Communication goal:** Introduce yourself, state your name, nationality, age, and job title in Danish
- **Grammar focus:** Present tense (er, hedder, kommer fra), personal pronouns (jeg, du, han, hun), basic SVO word order
- **Vocabulary focus:** Countries, nationalities, job titles, numbers 1–20, basic greetings (Hej, Goddag, Farvel)

---

### A1-02 — Numbers, Dates & Time
- **Theme:** Numerics and time expressions
- **Communication goal:** Say the time, give a date, use numbers in everyday context
- **Grammar focus:** Numbers 1–100, ordinal numbers, clock expressions (klokken er...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Days of the week, months, seasons, clock vocabulary, years

---

### A1-03 — Colors, Shapes & Describing Things
- **Theme:** Basic description
- **Communication goal:** Describe objects, colors, and basic physical properties
- **Grammar focus:** Adjective agreement (en/et/plural), indefinite articles (en/et), basic attributive use
- **Vocabulary focus:** Colors, shapes, sizes (stor/lille, lang/kort), common objects

---

### A1-04 — Family & Relationships
- **Theme:** Family and social circle
- **Communication goal:** Talk about your family members and describe relationships
- **Grammar focus:** Possessives (min, din, hans, hendes, vores), gender agreement with possessives
- **Vocabulary focus:** Family members, relationships (kæreste, ven, kollega), simple verbs (have, bo, arbejde)

---

### A1-05 — Where You Live
- **Theme:** Home, city, neighborhood
- **Communication goal:** Say where you live, describe your home and neighborhood, name key places in a city
- **Grammar focus:** Prepositions of place (i, på, ved, over, under, foran, bag), definite nouns (hunden, bilen)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Rooms in a home, types of housing, city places (butik, station, park, rådhus)

---

### A1-06 — Daily Routine
- **Theme:** Habits and schedule
- **Communication goal:** Describe what you do on a typical day, from morning to evening
- **Grammar focus:** Frequency adverbs (altid, ofte, aldrig, normalt), time expressions (om morgenen, om aftenen), verb conjugation in present
- **Vocabulary focus:** Daily activity verbs (vågne, spise, køre, arbejde, sove), meal names, times of day

---

### A1-07 — Food & Eating Habits
- **Theme:** Food, drink, meals
- **Communication goal:** Talk about what you eat and drink, express preferences, handle basic food orders
- **Grammar focus:** Verb "at spise/drikke/kunne lide", negation with "ikke", simple expressions of preference
- **Vocabulary focus:** Common foods and drinks, meals (morgenmad, frokost, aftensmad), taste adjectives (sød, salt, bitter)

---

### A1-08 — Getting Around Town
- **Theme:** Transport and directions
- **Communication goal:** Ask for and understand directions, name forms of transport, buy a ticket
- **Grammar focus:** Question formation (Hvor er...? Hvordan kommer jeg til...?), imperatives (gå, tag, drej)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Transport types, direction words (til venstre, til højre, ligeud), landmarks

---

### A1-09 — Feelings & States
- **Theme:** Emotions and physical states
- **Communication goal:** Express how you feel, describe physical states, respond when someone asks "Hvordan har du det?"
- **Grammar focus:** Verb "at have det + adjective", "at være + adjective", basic stative expressions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Emotion adjectives (glad, ked af det, træt, nervøs, stolt), physical state words (sulten, tørstig, syg, rask)

---

### A1-10 — The Seasons & Weather
- **Theme:** Nature and weather
- **Communication goal:** Talk about the weather and seasons — a foundational Danish small-talk topic
- **Grammar focus:** Impersonal "det" (Det regner, Det blæser), weather verbs and adjectives
- **Vocabulary focus:** Weather words (sol, regn, sne, vind, tåge), temperature, season names

---

### A1-11 — Shopping Basics
- **Theme:** Retail and transactions
- **Communication goal:** Find items in a shop, ask for prices, complete a basic purchase
- **Grammar focus:** Question words (Hvad koster...? Har I...?), numbers in price context, "må jeg..." for polite requests
- **Vocabulary focus:** Shop types, product categories, transaction phrases (kvittering, kreditkort, rabat)

---

### A1-12 — Asking Questions & Getting Help
- **Theme:** Communication repair and help-seeking
- **Communication goal:** Ask someone to repeat or slow down, say you don't understand, ask how to say something in Danish
- **Grammar focus:** All question words (hvad, hvor, hvornår, hvorfor, hvem, hvordan), polite modal "kan du...?"
- **Vocabulary focus:** Meta-communication phrases (Kan du gentage det? Hvad hedder det på dansk? Jeg forstår ikke.)

---

## A2 — Building Blocks (23 modules)
*Core grammar expands: past tenses, future, connectors, and first exposure to professional Danish.*

---

### A2-01 — Talking About Your Job
- **Theme:** Work and profession (depth)
- **Communication goal:** Describe your job, your responsibilities, your workplace, and your colleagues
- **Grammar focus:** Present tense consolidation, "at arbejde som/med/i", profession vocabulary in context
- **Vocabulary focus:** Job titles, industries, workplace nouns (kontor, møde, rapport, chef, kollega), action verbs for professional tasks

---

### A2-02 — A Day in the Past (Simple Past)
- **Theme:** Narrating past events
- **Communication goal:** Describe what happened yesterday, last week, or in a past situation
- **Grammar focus:** Preterite (datid) of regular and key irregular verbs (var, gik, sagde, kom, fik, tog)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Past time expressions (i går, for en uge siden, sidst, dengang), narrative verbs

---

### A2-03 — What Have You Done? (Present Perfect)
- **Theme:** Experience and recent past
- **Communication goal:** Talk about life experiences and recent events using present perfect
- **Grammar focus:** Present perfect with "har" and "er" + past participle, choosing between preterite and perfect
- **Vocabulary focus:** Experience markers (nogensinde, aldrig, allerede, endnu), experiential verbs

---

### A2-04 — Plans & Intentions
- **Theme:** Future and intentions
- **Communication goal:** Talk about future plans, intentions, and obligations
- **Grammar focus:** Future with "skal", "vil", "kommer til at"; difference between skal (obligation) and vil (desire/will)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Future time expressions (i morgen, næste uge, om et år), planning verbs (planlægge, håbe, tænke at)

---

### A2-05 — Time, Duration & Temporal Reference
- **Theme:** Temporal grammar — one of the trickiest areas for English speakers
- **Communication goal:** Express when things happen, how long they last, since when they started, and how far in the future they are — using the correct Danish temporal construction in each case
- **Grammar focus:** The four core temporal patterns and when to use each:
  - *i + duration* → ongoing/completed duration ("jeg har boet her i ti år", "jeg arbejdede der i to år")
  - *siden + point in time* → starting point of an ongoing state ("jeg har lært dansk siden 2022", "siden jeg kom til Danmark")
  - *om + duration* → future distance ("om to uger", "om et år")
  - *for + duration + siden* → completed past distance ("for tre måneder siden", "for to år siden")
- **Vocabulary focus:** Time-unit nouns (minut, time, dag, uge, måned, år, årti), temporal adverbs (allerede, endnu, stadig, snart, netop), temporal prepositions in context

---

### A2-06 — Danish Home Life
- **Theme:** Home, household, and domestic life
- **Communication goal:** Describe your apartment or house, talk about household tasks, discuss rent and living arrangements
- **Grammar focus:** Double definiteness (det store hus, den røde dør), adjective inflection in definite form
- **Vocabulary focus:** Furniture, appliances, household tasks (gøre rent, vaske tøj, lave mad), housing types

---

### A2-07 — Health & the Body
- **Theme:** Medical and health
- **Communication goal:** Describe symptoms, make a doctor's appointment, navigate basic Danish healthcare
- **Grammar focus:** Reflexive expressions (at føle sig, at slå sig), body part vocabulary with definiteness
- **Vocabulary focus:** Body parts, symptoms (feber, hoste, ondt i...),  healthcare nouns (læge, recept, apoteket)

---

### A2-08 — Shopping & Money
- **Theme:** Financial transactions and retail
- **Communication goal:** Compare prices, ask about sizes and availability, exchange or return items
- **Grammar focus:** Comparative adjectives (større, billigere, bedre), modal "kan jeg..." for requests
- **Vocabulary focus:** Shopping vocabulary, currencies, price comparison phrases, Danish payment culture (MobilePay)

---

### A2-09 — Danish Traditions & Holidays
- **Theme:** Cultural calendar
- **Communication goal:** Discuss Danish holidays, traditions, and seasonal celebrations with colleagues
- **Grammar focus:** Temporal expressions with definite form (om julen, til påske), verbs of tradition (fejre, give, samles)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Danish holidays (jul, påske, pinse, st. hans), tradition nouns, gift and food vocabulary

---

### A2-10 — Connecting Your Thoughts
- **Theme:** Conjunctions and sentence linking
- **Communication goal:** Express cause, contrast, condition, and time using subordinate connectors
- **Grammar focus:** Subordinating conjunctions (fordi, selvom, hvis, når, mens, indtil, efter at, før), basic subordinate clause structure
- **Vocabulary focus:** The connectors themselves as productive items, transition phrases

---

### A2-11 — Talking About Experiences
- **Theme:** Experiences, recommendations, advice
- **Communication goal:** Ask about and share experiences, recommend places and activities, give and receive advice
- **Grammar focus:** "Har du prøvet...?", modal "burde" for advice, "man kan..." for general recommendations
- **Vocabulary focus:** Recommendation phrases, place types (museum, restaurant, koncert), adjectives for evaluation

---

### A2-12 — Eating Out in Denmark
- **Theme:** Restaurants and food culture
- **Communication goal:** Order a full meal, ask about dishes, handle the bill, make small talk at a business lunch
- **Grammar focus:** Polite requests ("Jeg vil gerne have...", "Kan jeg få..."), "at smage", past tense for what you enjoyed
- **Vocabulary focus:** Menu vocabulary, dish types, dietary preferences, dining etiquette phrases

---

### A2-13 — Travel & Getting There
- **Theme:** Travel and logistics
- **Communication goal:** Plan a trip, buy tickets, check into accommodation, handle travel disruptions
- **Grammar focus:** Verbs of movement (rejse, flyve, køre, ankomme, afgå), future tense for planning, conditionals (hvis tog er forsinket...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Transport modes, travel verbs, accommodation vocabulary, Danish geography (Jylland, Fyn, Sjælland, Bornholm)

---

### A2-14 — Small Talk: The Danish Way
- **Theme:** Social conversation and cultural norms
- **Communication goal:** Hold a natural 3–5 minute small talk conversation with a Danish colleague on neutral topics
- **Grammar focus:** Inversion (topic-fronting: I dag har jeg...), question tags and confirmation expressions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Classic Danish small talk topics (vejret, ferie, weekenden, sport, børn), hedging phrases (egentlig, faktisk, ret)

---

### A2-15 — Describing People
- **Theme:** Physical and personality descriptions
- **Communication goal:** Describe what someone looks like and what they are like as a person
- **Grammar focus:** Comparative and superlative adjectives, word order with multiple adjectives, "at virke som"
- **Vocabulary focus:** Physical descriptors, personality adjectives (venlig, direkte, ambitiøs, rolig), nationality and origin

---

### A2-16 — The Danish Media (Basics)
- **Theme:** News and media
- **Communication goal:** Understand simple news headlines, talk about a news story with a colleague
- **Grammar focus:** Passive voice (basics — "det blev besluttet"), formal register vs. spoken
- **Vocabulary focus:** News vocabulary (nyhed, regering, valg, krise), media sources (Berlingske, Politiken, DR)

---

### A2-17 — Public Services
- **Theme:** Civic and bureaucratic life
- **Communication goal:** Interact with Danish public institutions: bank, municipality (kommune), post office
- **Grammar focus:** Formal polite requests, phone and written communication openers
- **Vocabulary focus:** Civic vocabulary (CPR-nummer, NemID/MitID, folkeregister, kommune), bureaucratic process words

---

### A2-18 — Opinions & Preferences
- **Theme:** Expressing views and preferences
- **Communication goal:** State opinions, agree and disagree, express preferences with reasons
- **Grammar focus:** Opinion phrases ("Jeg synes...", "Jeg mener...", "Efter min mening..."), contrast connectors (men, selvom, på den anden side)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Opinion and preference vocabulary, agreement/disagreement phrases, hedging adverbs (egentlig, nok, vel)

---

### A2-19 — Basic Business Interactions
- **Theme:** Professional communication basics
- **Communication goal:** Book a meeting, confirm an appointment, write a simple professional email in Danish
- **Grammar focus:** Formal written register (De vs. du), modal phrases for politeness ("Jeg tillader mig at..."), email conventions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Meeting vocabulary, calendar phrases, email opening/closing formulas, basic corporate vocabulary

---

### A2-20 — Hobbies, Sports & Leisure
- **Theme:** Free time and personal interests
- **Communication goal:** Talk about your hobbies and the sports you play or follow; understand and join a conversation about how Danes spend their free time
- **Grammar focus:** Habitual present ("jeg løber", "jeg spiller fodbold"), "at dyrke" + activity, frequency adverbs revisited in leisure context
- **Vocabulary focus:** Common sports (fodbold, håndbold, cykling, svømning, løb), hobby categories (madlavning, fotografering, musik, håndværk, havebrug), leisure verbs (dyrke, spille, se, følge med i, gå til)

---

### A2-21 — Making Plans & Social Invitations
- **Theme:** Social arrangements and invitations
- **Communication goal:** Invite someone to do something, accept or decline an invitation, arrange a time and place to meet, and follow up on social plans
- **Grammar focus:** Future with "vil" and "skal" for social plans, time prepositions (på lørdag, om fredagen, klokken...), polite decline formulas
- **Vocabulary focus:** Invitation phrases (Har du lyst til...?, Vil du med...?, Skal vi ikke...?), acceptance and decline phrases (Det lyder skønt!, Desværre kan jeg ikke), social plan vocabulary (aftale, mødes, ses, aflyse, rykke)

---

### A2-22 — Texting & Informal Digital Communication
- **Theme:** Digital language and informal written Danish
- **Communication goal:** Write natural, informal Danish messages — texts, WhatsApp, social media comments — without sounding over-formal or stilted
- **Grammar focus:** Ellipsis in informal writing (pronoun drop, short fragments), spoken-register features in writing, informal punctuation norms
- **Vocabulary focus:** Common informal written phrases (Hej!, Vi ses!, Skal vi ikke bare...?, Det er fedt!), Danish texting shorthand (ikk', nå, øh, haha), group chat vocabulary (gruppechat, svare, videresende, slette), emoji conventions in Danish digital culture

---

### A2-23 — Danish TV, Music & Entertainment
- **Theme:** Danish popular culture and media
- **Communication goal:** Recommend and discuss a Danish TV show, film, or music artist; understand and join small talk about popular culture; navigate the Danish streaming and media landscape
- **Grammar focus:** Present perfect for recommendations ("Har du set...?", "Har du hørt...?"), opinion phrases with entertainment content, "synes om" vs. "kan lide" revisited
- **Vocabulary focus:** Danish TV and streaming (DR, TV2, afsnit, sæson, serie, streame), key shows as cultural touchstones (Borgen, Forbrydelsen, Badehotellet), music genres and Danish artists, review vocabulary (anmeldelse, premiere, nomineret, Bodilprisen)

---

## B1 — Entering the Workplace (24 modules)
*The professional pivot. Grammar consolidates around complex sentences; workplace and social Danish are treated as primary domains.*

---

### B1-01 — The Danish Workplace
- **Theme:** Office culture and professional norms
- **Communication goal:** Navigate Danish work culture: flat hierarchy, direct communication style, consensus decision-making, work-life balance
- **Grammar focus:** Consolidation of workplace register, contrast between formal and informal professional Danish
- **Vocabulary focus:** Workplace culture words (konsensus, tillid, ansvar, feedback, fleksibel), the language of Danish organizational life (Janteloven references, hygge at work)

---

### B1-02 — Participating in Meetings (Basics)
- **Theme:** Meetings and professional discourse
- **Communication goal:** Follow and contribute to a basic Danish business meeting: agree, clarify, ask questions, summarize
- **Grammar focus:** Inversion for emphasis ("Det mener jeg også", "Det er jeg enig i"), question formation in formal register
- **Vocabulary focus:** Meeting phrases (dagsorden, referat, punkter, opfølgning), turn-taking phrases (Må jeg sige noget?, Jeg vil gerne tilføje...), agreement/disagreement softeners

---

### B1-03 — Professional Email 101
- **Theme:** Business writing — email
- **Communication goal:** Write a professional email in correct Danish: request, inform, follow up, decline politely
- **Grammar focus:** Formal subordinate clause structure, passive for impersonality, formal modal verbs (bedes, anmodes om)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Email formulas (Kære, Venlig hilsen, Med venlig hilsen, Jeg skriver vedrørende...), professional action verbs in written register

---

### B1-04 — Describing Work Processes
- **Theme:** Procedures and processes
- **Communication goal:** Explain how a process works, walk someone through a procedure step by step
- **Grammar focus:** Sequential connectors (først, derefter, så, til sidst, når... så...), infinitive constructions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Process verbs (implementere, godkende, vurdere, tilpasse), Danish business process vocabulary

---

### B1-05 — Numbers, Data & Statistics in Context
- **Theme:** Quantitative professional communication
- **Communication goal:** Present and discuss figures, percentages, trends, and comparisons in a professional context
- **Grammar focus:** Comparatives in professional register, "er steget/faldet med X%", nominalization (a rise = en stigning)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Statistical and financial vocabulary (vækst, fald, andel, gennemsnit, mål, resultat), trend language (stige, falde, stagnere, toppe)

---

### B1-06 — Danish Society & How It Works
- **Theme:** Danish civic and political structure
- **Communication goal:** Discuss the Danish welfare state, political system, and public institutions with colleagues
- **Grammar focus:** Present and past tense in descriptive register, passive constructions for institutional language
- **Vocabulary focus:** Civic vocabulary (folketing, minister, parti, kommune, region, velfærdsstat, skat), political institutions

---

### B1-07 — Discussing Problems & Finding Solutions
- **Theme:** Problem-solving discourse
- **Communication goal:** Raise a problem, propose a solution, evaluate options, and reach agreement in Danish
- **Grammar focus:** Problem-solution connectors (problemet er at..., en mulig løsning er..., det ville betyde at...), conditional with "hvis"
- **Vocabulary focus:** Problem vocabulary (udfordring, risiko, konsekvens, løsning, kompromis), solution-framing language

---

### B1-08 — Agreeing, Disagreeing & Hedging
- **Theme:** Opinion and stance in professional discourse
- **Communication goal:** Express nuanced agreement or disagreement, hedge a position, acknowledge another viewpoint before challenging it
- **Grammar focus:** Concessive clauses (selvom, ganske vist... men, på trods af at), modal adverbs (nok, vel, jo, da)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Stance markers (Jeg er delvis enig, Det er en pointe, men..., Jeg ser det lidt anderledes...), Danish hedging culture

---

### B1-09 — Narrating & Storytelling
- **Theme:** Complex past narratives
- **Communication goal:** Tell a story about a past event with setting, sequence, and resolution — including what happened and how you felt
- **Grammar focus:** Preterite vs. perfect in narrative, past progressive equivalents, narrative connectors (pludselig, derefter, til sidst, det viste sig at...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Narrative verbs, emotional response vocabulary, sequencing adverbs

---

### B1-10 — Danish Current Events
- **Theme:** News and current affairs
- **Communication goal:** Read a Danish news article, understand the main points, and discuss it with colleagues
- **Grammar focus:** Passive in news register, reported speech (sagde at, meddelte at), nominalization patterns in formal press
- **Vocabulary focus:** Political and economic news vocabulary, Danish media outlets, current affairs nouns (lovforslag, reform, aftale, krise)

---

### B1-11 — Subordinate Clause Mastery
- **Theme:** Grammar deep-dive — sentence structure
- **Communication goal:** Construct and understand complex sentences with correct subordinate clause word order (the V2 reversal)
- **Grammar focus:** Adverbial placement in subordinate clauses (negation "ikke" before verb), embedded clauses (at, om, fordi, selvom, hvis + subject + adverb + verb), indirect questions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Complex connector set; grammatical meta-vocabulary to discuss sentence structure

---

### B1-12 — Conditionals & Hypotheticals
- **Theme:** Hypothetical and conditional language
- **Communication goal:** Express what would happen if..., discuss scenarios, speculate about alternatives
- **Grammar focus:** Real conditionals (hvis... så...), hypothetical conditionals (hvis jeg var..., ville jeg...), "burde have + past participle" for past regret
- **Vocabulary focus:** Hypothetical markers (tænk hvis, forestil dig at, i så fald), speculation verbs (antage, forestille sig, forudsætte)

---

### B1-13 — Polite Requests & Polite Refusals
- **Theme:** Register and politeness
- **Communication goal:** Make requests, decline invitations or requests, and handle uncomfortable situations gracefully in Danish professional and social settings
- **Grammar focus:** Modal hierarchy for politeness (kan, vil, må, kunne, ville), subjunctive-adjacent constructions (det ville jeg sætte pris på)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Politeness vocabulary, softening phrases (desværre, jeg er bange for at, det ville jeg ønske men...), Danish directness vs. formal hedging

---

### B1-14 — Phone & Video Call Language
- **Theme:** Spoken professional communication
- **Communication goal:** Open and close calls, identify yourself, take messages, and handle the mechanics of a video call in Danish
- **Grammar focus:** Spoken Danish register, discourse management phrases, clarification questions
- **Vocabulary focus:** Phone/call vocabulary (Hej, det er X fra Y, Kan jeg tale med...?, Jeg ringer vedrørende..., Viderekoble, besked), video call conventions

---

### B1-15 — Danish Culture & Values
- **Theme:** Cultural literacy
- **Communication goal:** Discuss Danish cultural values — hygge, Janteloven, directness, work-life balance — fluently enough to engage authentically with Danish colleagues
- **Grammar focus:** Expressing abstract concepts, generalizations with "man", cultural commentary register
- **Vocabulary focus:** Key cultural concepts and their nuance (tillid, fællesskab, ligestilling, forhandlingskultur, velfærd), names for cultural phenomena

---

### B1-16 — Healthcare & the Danish System
- **Theme:** Health and public services
- **Communication goal:** Register with a GP, describe symptoms clearly, understand advice, discuss the Danish healthcare model
- **Grammar focus:** Complex description of ongoing conditions, temporal clauses with "siden" and "i + time expression"
- **Vocabulary focus:** Medical vocabulary (diagnose, symptom, undersøgelse, recept, alment praktiserende læge, sygehusvæsen), Danish health system nouns

---

### B1-17 — Business Travel & Hosting
- **Theme:** Travel for work, hosting visitors
- **Communication goal:** Welcome a foreign guest, give a city/office tour in Danish, navigate travel logistics, make reservations
- **Grammar focus:** Hosting language (Velkommen til..., Må jeg præsentere..., Lad mig vise dig...), future planning structures
- **Vocabulary focus:** Hospitality vocabulary, logistics vocabulary (reservation, transport, overnatning), Denmark geography and key cities

---

### B1-18 — Talking About Qualifications & Experience
- **Theme:** Professional background and CV language
- **Communication goal:** Describe your career history, education, and competencies — useful for introductions in new professional contexts
- **Grammar focus:** Present perfect for experience (jeg har arbejdet med... i X år), relative clauses (den stilling, som jeg...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** CV vocabulary (uddannelse, erfaring, kompetence, stilling, branche), professional narrative connectors

---

### B1-19 — Danish Legal & Administrative Basics
- **Theme:** Legal and bureaucratic language
- **Communication goal:** Understand a basic Danish administrative letter, contract clause, or legal notice; know what questions to ask
- **Grammar focus:** Passive constructions in formal writing, nominalization, impersonal constructions ("Det er hermed bekræftet at...")
- **Vocabulary focus:** Administrative vocabulary (aftale, vilkår, ophør, opsigelse, frist, ansøgning, tilladelse), key legal terms

---

### B1-20 — Expressing Cause, Effect & Consequence
- **Theme:** Logical connectors — advanced
- **Communication goal:** Construct professional arguments and causal chains with precision
- **Grammar focus:** Advanced connectors (idet, dermed, således, følgelig, som følge af, på grund af, med henblik på), nominalizations
- **Vocabulary focus:** Logical relationship vocabulary, cause-effect nouns (årsag, konsekvens, virkning, resultat, indvirkning)

---

### B1-21 — Foreningsliv: Clubs, Associations & Volunteering
- **Theme:** Danish association culture
- **Communication goal:** Join a sports club, hobby group, or civic association; participate in a foreningsbestyrelse meeting; discuss volunteering commitments and organizational roles; understand why foreningsliv is structurally important in Danish social life
- **Grammar focus:** Formal meeting language in the informal register typical of associations (contrast with corporate meeting language), modal verbs for voluntary obligation (vi plejer at..., det er frivilligt, man kan godt...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Association vocabulary (forening, idrætsforening, bestyrelse, kontingent, frivillig, generalforsamling, vedtægter, formand, næstformand, kasserer, sekretær), volunteering vocabulary (melde sig ind, melde sig ud, bidrage, engagere sig), common Danish association types (sportsklub, spejder, kor, grundejerforening)

---

### B1-22 — Parenting & Family Life in Denmark
- **Theme:** Danish childhood institutions and parenting culture
- **Communication goal:** Navigate daily conversations around children's lives — daycare, school, after-school care — and participate in a forældremøde; understand the Danish approach to childhood and parenting norms
- **Grammar focus:** Describing institutional routines (temporal clauses, frequency adverbs), polite semi-formal communication with institutions (writing to a teacher, addressing a pædagog)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Childcare vocabulary (vuggestue, børnehave, dagpleje, SFO, fritidsordning, pædagog), school vocabulary (folkeskole, klasse, klasselærer, skolebestyrelse, forældremøde, karakterer, trivselsmåling), parenting culture words (frihed under ansvar, udeliv, selvstændighed, lekser)

---

### B1-23 — Neighborhood Life & Local Civic Participation
- **Theme:** Local community and civic engagement
- **Communication goal:** Participate in a grundejerforening or ejerforening meeting, engage with neighbors about shared concerns, navigate a beboermøde, and understand local democratic structures
- **Grammar focus:** Formal-informal hybrid register of local meetings, requests and objections in civic context, passive for bylaws and shared rules (det er ikke tilladt at..., det er besluttet at..., fællesarealer må ikke...)
- **Vocabulary focus:** Housing association vocabulary (grundejerforening, ejerforening, andelsboligforening, bestyrelse, vedligeholdelse, fællesarealer, støjklage, husorden), neighborhood vocabulary (nabo, nabohjælp, hæk, parkeringsplads, renovation), local democracy vocabulary (kommunalbestyrelse, lokalplan, høring, borgermøde, indsigelse)

---

- **Vocabulary focus:** Business case vocabulary (businesscase, investeringsforslag, ROI, tilbagebetalingstid, nytteværdi, risikovurdering, alternativer, anbefaling), financial justification vocabulary (omkostningsbesparelse, effektiviseringsgevinst, vækstpotentiale, strategisk værdi, konkurrencefordel), decision vocabulary (godkendelse, bevilling, sanktionering, prioritering, go/no-go)

## C2 — Mastery (15 modules)
*Refinement, personal style, and native-level authority. The gap between C1 and C2 is style, not grammar.*

### C2-01 — Native Patterns & Natural Discourse
- **Theme:** Spontaneous native-level speech
- **Communication goal:** Sustain extended, unscripted conversation at native-speaker pace and depth — matching the natural discourse flow of an educated Danish professional
- **Grammar focus:** Spoken grammar patterns (topic-comment structures, incomplete sentences, repair strategies), natural ellipsis
- **Vocabulary focus:** High-frequency native patterns that are rare in textbooks, natural filler strategies

### C2-02 — The Language of Authority & Leadership
- **Theme:** How leaders speak in Danish
- **Communication goal:** Command a room in Danish — with presence, precision, and the tone that signals authority without aggression
- **Grammar focus:** Sentence-final focus, syntactic brevity for impact, directive without softening when appropriate

## Notes on Sequencing

### Pedagogical arc across the six levels

| Level | Focus | Register | Professional load |
|-------|--------|----------|-------------------|
| A1 | Survival and foundation | Spoken, informal | None |
| A2 | Everyday life + first professional exposure | Mixed | Light |
| B1 | Workplace entry; complex grammar | Professional basics | Medium |
| B2 | Full professional fluency; leadership prep | Formal and informal | Heavy |
| C1 | Executive and strategic | High formal + cultural | Very heavy |
| C2 | Style, authority, native nuance | All registers mastered | Director level |

### Grammar sequencing rationale
- **A1–A2:** Present → past → future; basic clause structure; agreement
- **B1:** Subordinate clause word order (the hardest structural rule for English speakers); conditionals; complex connectors
- **B2:** Passive; nominalization; register switching; discourse markers
- **C1–C2:** Rhetoric; style; sociolinguistic precision; pragmatic particles

### Vocabulary load estimate
| Level | New items (approx.) | Cumulative |
|-------|---------------------|------------|
| A1 | 400 | 400 |
| A2 | 600 | 1,000 |
| B1 | 700 | 1,700 |
| B2 | 800 | 2,500 |
| C1 | 900 | 3,400 |
| C2 | 600 | 4,000 |

*4,000 vocabulary items covers active professional use at director level. Most educated native speakers have a passive vocabulary of 15,000–20,000 words — but the active professional core is well within this range.*
